Why Build a Prototype

Save Time

Building prototypes are always faster than development. The more you learn while prototyping, the faster the implementation.

More Iterations

You can make your product better by prototyping and iterating many times because its quicker, faster, and cheaper than full development.

Save Money

Prototyping is cheaper than full development and it shortens the overall development cycle by allowing you to get a product in your customer's hands sooner.

Get Feedback Quickly

The sooner you can get a product in stakeholders hands, the sooner you can get feedback.

Get Started Quickly

Prototyping allows you to avoid procrastination and get started quicker.

Understand the Problem Better

Prototyping helps to understand the problem better through trial, research, and implementation.

How it Works

Describe Your Product

Hear Back from our Developers

Within 3 days, you'll recieve a quote for:

  • Price.
  • Estimated date of completion.
  • Full list of features for the prototype.
  • Technical details (libraries used, hosting, etc.).
  • A list of criteria agreed upon which must be met for a prototype to be considered "done".

Negotiate and Accept Terms

Next, you will respond back with any concerns and changes to the prototype implementation and features. Once a conclusion has been reached and a mutually agreed upon down payment has been payed, the developers will start working on your prototype.

Get Updates from Developers

While the developers work on your prototype, you'll recieve updates on progress. You can also message us to ask questions or further clarify your prototype.

Recieve the Finished Prototype

When the project is finished you'll recieve:

  • The completed prototype.
  • A zip file containing the final source code.
  • Instructions for use of the prototype.
  • The ability to submit revisions and feedback.

Who should prototype?

Everybody! Prototyping has been around for hundreds of years in all different areas of life. It helps discover barriers in implementation as well as create a product that will be useful to your customers.

How are bugs found after final implementation fixed?

If the bug causes "done" criteria to fail for any of the features agreed upon for the prototype, it will be fixed for free, anytime. If the bug is outside of the feature set, it can be discussed will your developer (depending on the size of the bug, we are usually pretty understanding).
